- [ ] Tune-up: cables, brake pads, chain

## Day by Day

---

### June 29 — Arrive Paris

Get to hotel. Eat something. Sleep.

**Sleep:** Paris — Marais, Montmartre, or Bastille
Boutique €100–180 · Generator Paris hostel ~€30–40/dorm

---

### June 30 — Paris Tourist Day

Leave the bike at the hotel. Métro day pass ~€16.

**Morning**
- **Notre-Dame** — fully reopened Dec 2024. Free to enter. Walk across to Île de la Cité.
- **Sainte-Chapelle** — 100m from Notre-Dame inside the Palais de Justice. Best Gothic stained glass in the world. ~€15. Don't miss it.
- Walk Pont Neuf back across the Seine

**Midday**
- **Le Marais** (3e/4e) — **Place des Vosges**, Rue des Rosiers (falafel). Lunch: L'As du Fallafel or any brasserie with *le plat du jour*.

**Afternoon — pick one or two**
- **Trocadéro** — free Eiffel Tower view across the Seine, no queue
- **Musée de Cluny** — small medieval art museum; the Lady and the Unicorn tapestries
- **Canal Saint-Martin** (10e) — iron footbridges, locks, zero tourists

**Evening**
- Métro to **Montmartre** / **Sacré-Coeur** for sunset. All of Paris below you.
- Dinner in Montmartre

> Most major museums closed Monday — check before visiting any specific institution.

---

### July 1 — Paris → Cahors

**Station:** Paris **Gare d'Austerlitz** (not Gare de Lyon or Gare du Nord) — Métro line 5 or 10.

- **Intercités → Cahors** ~5.5h · ~€20–25 ticket + €10 bike (luggage van — already reserved)
- Buy food before boarding

**Arrive Cahors, late afternoon**
- Walk to **Pont Valentré** — UNESCO World Heritage medieval bridge, 2 min from city center. Worth 30 min at dusk.
- Stock up for tomorrow's ride: fill bottles, buy food. This is the last full city before the festival.

**Sleep:** Cahors (see lodging table above)

---

### July 2 — Cahors → Festival (~55 km)

**Route:** V86 Véloroute Vallée du Lot — signed, paved, follows the river.
Check conditions before departure: [cahorsvalleedulot.com](https://www.cahorsvalleedulot.com/explorer/evadez-vous-sur-les-chemins/la-veloroute-de-la-vallee-du-lot/)

**Start by 7:30 AM** — July hits 35°C+ by midday.

| Stop | ~Km | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| Vers | 15 | Gorge narrows, cliffs close in. Water fountain. |
| **Bouzies** | 30 | Towpath carved directly into the cliff face for barge horses. Walk a section. |
| **Saint-Cirq-Lapopie** ⭐ | 25–30 | Medieval village on a 100m limestone cliff above the river. Short steep climb off the road. Café + ice cream at the top. 45–60 min. Don't skip. |
| **Cajarc** | 40 | Last real stop. Café, boulangerie. Top up everything — nothing between here and the festival. |
| Festival | 55 | Set up camp. |

**Sleep:** On-site camping (RA.co) · Backup: Camping Le Terriol, Cajarc

---

### July 6 — Festival → Cahors → Angoulême

**Morning:** 55 km back to Cahors on the V86 (same road in reverse)

**Afternoon:** Train Cahors → Angoulême via Agen + Bordeaux (~4.5h · ~€30–40 + bike, 1–2 changes)

### July 7 — Angoulême Rest Day

Girlfriend takes TGV **Paris Montparnasse → Angoulême** (~2h10, ~€30–60).

**See:**
- **Cité Internationale de la Bande Dessinée** — world-class comics museum, whole city covered in murals
- Walk the ramparts — city sits on a promontory, views over the Charente valley

**Sleep:** Angoulême

---

### July 8 — Angoulême → Poitiers (~100 km)

**Angoulême exit:** Head south via Soyaux/La Couronne — flattest way out of the city (promontory climb otherwise).
**Heat:** Start by 7 AM. Open plateau, no shade, potential headwinds. Carry 2L minimum.

| Stop | ~Km | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| Ruffec | 45 | Boulangerie, café. Lunch. |
| Villages | every 15–20 km | Water fountain in each square |

**Sleep:** Poitiers — ibis budget Poitiers Centre Gare (**bike shelter**)

---

### July 9 — Poitiers → Tours (~100 km)

| Stop | ~Km | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| Villages | every 15–20 km | Water fountain in each square |
| **Châtellerault** | ~75 | Good lunch town, last real stop before Tours |
| Tours | ~100 | Full city, everything available |

**Sleep:** Tours — The People Hostel (**Accueil Vélo**) · Book early.

---

### July 10 — Tours → Blois (~65 km)

**Route:** D952 Tours → Amboise → Blois — paved, Loire-hugging. Ignore the "Loire à Vélo" gravel route.

| Stop | ~Km | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Amboise** ⭐ | 25 | **Clos Lucé** — Leonardo da Vinci's home, died here 1519; gardens with bronze models of his inventions. 1.5–2 hours. Also: **Château d'Amboise** above the town. Café, boulangerie, full stop. |
| **Chaumont-sur-Loire** | 42 | **Festival International des Jardins** runs Apr 22–Nov 1, 2026 — 30 international designers on château grounds. Right on the D952. [domaine-chaumont.fr](https://domaine-chaumont.fr/) Café. |
| **Blois** | 55 | Major town, everything available. **Château Royal de Blois** — four wings, four centuries; Catherine de Médicis's hidden cabinet rooms. |

**Sleep:** Blois — Éthic Étapes Val de Loire (**Accueil Vélo**) · Book early.

> **Amboise overnight option:** Night at Camping Île d'Or (~€12–18), then easy 40 km to Blois next morning.

---

### July 11 — Blois → Orléans (~60 km)

**Route:** D951 Blois → Beaugency → Orléans — paved, Loire-hugging.

| Stop | ~Km | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Chambord** *(optional detour)* | +15 off route | Largest Loire château. 440 rooms. Double-helix staircase. If doing one detour, this is it. |
| **Beaugency** | 40 | Tour de César — massive Romanesque keep standing loose in the town square, 200 years older than the châteaux. Café. |
| **Orléans** | 60 | Full city. **Cathédrale Sainte-Croix** — stained glass tells the full Joan of Arc story around the nave. |

**Sleep:** Orléans — Auberge de Jeunesse d'Orléans

---

### July 12 — Orléans → Paris

**Don't bike this.** Train instead.

- **Orléans → Paris Austerlitz TER** — ~1h · ~€15–20 + bike (luggage van, already reserved)
- Early train arrives Paris ~8:15 AM

**Paris: last day**
- Leave bikes at hotel, walk the city
- Best neighborhoods: Marais, Saint-Germain, Montmartre
- Dinner: pick somewhere good — you're done riding

**Sleep:** Paris splurge hotel. Book 2–3 months ahead.

---

### July 13 — Fly Home

- CDG from central Paris: RER B, ~45 min
- Allow 2.5–3 hours before departure for bike check-in

## Reference

### Tour de France 2026 — Possible Detour ⭐

TDF runs July 4–26. **Stage 7 finishes in Bordeaux, July 10** — legendary sprint finish in the city center. Your return route goes through Bordeaux on July 6; Stage 7 is the 10th, so you'd have passed through by then. If you want to stay and watch, you'd need 4 extra days in Bordeaux. Your call.

---

### Single Speed Notes

- Lot Valley → Dordogne direction: limestone causse, brutal climbing. This itinerary avoids it (train to Angoulême instead).
- **Gear ratio:** 42×16 or 42×17 ideal. The Loire section is flat; you'll spin fine.

---

### General Tips

- **Start by 7 AM** in July. Stop 12–3 PM. Resume afternoon.
- Boulangeries open 7–7:30 AM. Closed Sunday afternoon + one weekday.
- Tabacs sell snacks, maps, phone credit.
- Village squares have free water fountains (*fontaine d'eau potable*).
- Intermarché / Leclerc on town outskirts for bulk resupply.
- *"Une carafe d'eau"* at any café = free tap water.
- Accueil Vélo hosts (700+) searchable at francevelotourisme.com.
- **GPX:** Route on Komoot to D952/D951 directly — not the auto-suggested "Loire à Vélo" option.
